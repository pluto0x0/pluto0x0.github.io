Distributed System (6-7)

linearization

  • Liveness: guarantee that something good will happen eventually
  • Safety: guarantee that something bad will never happen.
  • Stable: once true, stays true forever afterwards

Multicast

Basic Multicast (B-Multicast)

  • B-multicast(group ggg , message mmm ):
    • for each process ppp in ggg , send (p,m)(p,m)(p,m) .
  • receive( mmm ): B-deliver( mmm ) at ppp

Reliable Multicast (R-Multicast)

  • Integrity: A correct process ppp delivers a message mmm at most once.
  • Validity: If a correct process multicasts (sends) message mmm , then it will eventually deliver mmm to itself. (Liveness)
  • Agreement: If a correct process delivers message mmm , then all the other correct processes in group( mmm ) will eventually deliver mmm .
  • Validity and agreement together ensure overall liveness: if some
    correct process multicasts a message m, then, all correct processes
    deliver m too.

R-multicast is reliable even after the sender crashes.

Ordered Multicast

FIFO ordering

For a process, if it sends m1m_1m1​ before m2m_2m2​ , then all processes recieves m1m_1m1​ before m2m_2m2​ .

Causal ordering

If multicasts m1→m2m_1 \to m_2m1​→m2​ ( m1m_1m1​ cause m2m_2m2​ , which means a process sends m2m_2m2​ after recieving m1m_1m1​ ), then all processes recieves m1m_1m1​ before m2m_2m2​ .

Causal ordering implies FIFO ordering, because multicasts that sent by a process can be regarded as Causal

Total ordering

For any pair of multicasts m1m_1m1​ , m2m_2m2​ , all processes recieving them recieves them in the same order.

Total Ordering doesn’t imply Causal Ordering

ISIS algorithm for total ordering

Sender multicasts message to everyone.

Each message recieved by a process may have:

  • agreed priority
  • proposed priority
  • a marker: undeliverable or not

Each processes PiP_iPi​ have

  • largest agreed priority aia_iai​
  • largest proposed priority pip_ipi​
  • an priority queue of messages order by agreed priority or proposed priority

To send a total order multicast mmm to all processes in ggg by PiP_iPi​ :

  • B-multicast (g,{m,id})(g, \{ m, id\})(g,{m,id}) where ididid is an identifier for mmm .

Proof of ISIS algorithm

two messages: m1m_1m1​ , m2m_2m2​ , two procesess ppp , qqq .
Assume ppp delivers m1m_1m1​ first.

When ppp delivers m1m_1m1​ , m2m_2m2​ is either

  • in queue and deliverable.
    • agreed_priority (m1)(m_1)(m1​) <<< agreed_priority (m2)(m_2)(m2​)

    • agreed_priority (m1)(m_1)(m1​) <<< propose_priority (m2)(m_2)(m2​) ≤\le≤ agreed_priority(m2)(m_2)(m2​)
  • not in queue.
    • agreed_priority (m2)(m_2)(m2​) ≥\ge≥ propose_priority (m2)(m_2)(m2​) >>> agreed_priority (m1)(m_1)(m1​)

Therefore
agreed_priority (m1)(m_1)(m1​) <<< agreed_priority (m2)(m_2)(m2​)
